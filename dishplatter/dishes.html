Ghanaian Dishes and Its Origin

The Origin Of Jollof Rice

Jollof rice s a very popular dish in Ghana; according to sauce, it originated in Senegal, and it is now prepared across the country by all the ethnic groups in Ghana with multiple recipes and methods for making it. However, so far, no ethinic group in Ghana has claimed or challenged its orignation becuase jollof rice taste differently in regards ingredients and recipes.

The Origin of Waakye

Waakye is usually eaten by Ghanaians as breakfast and is normally unique and prepared by the Hausa who migrated to Ghana. The main ingredients used are rice and beans. Due to its popularity, it has been adapted by almost all the ethnic groups in Ghana. Fufu, originally unique to the Akan ethnic group, is made from cassava and plantains as the main ingredients and is normally eaten with soup. Fufu is one of the most popular Ghanaian dishes, and all other ethnic groups prepare it now.

The Origin of Kenkey

While Ghanaian dishes are unique to each ethnic group, others are also common among the ethnic groups. Banku and kenkey (Ga kenkey) are unique to the Ga Adamgbe ethnic groups; these dishes are prepared using corn dough as an ingredient. The Fanti tribe also has kenkey (Fanti kenkey) as their unique dish, but it is made using a different method and material; however, they use the same ingredient as the Ga kenkey.
